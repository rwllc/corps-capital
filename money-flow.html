Financial Execution & Controls

How Money Moves

One person is accountable for getting payments out on time and in the right amount — protected by a clear, fraud-resistant approval flow.

The Principle

Tommy approves. Jasmine executes. Payments happen on time and in the right amounts — and if an answer isn't known, Jasmine goes to the source (Tino, the custodian, the bank) and resolves it rather than waiting.

The Control Protocol (every wire)

  1. Request logged — who, what, amount, purpose. Documented.
  2. Approval #1 — Tommy authorizes the movement (the decision).
  3. Approval #2 — Tino confirms operational sanity and funds.
  4. Verbal confirmation on a known channel before send — never email-only.
  5. Execution — Jasmine sends it.
  6. Acknowledgment back to Tommy, and an entry in the daily report.
This two-approval-plus-verbal standard is the safeguard the firm adopted after an earlier wire-fraud incident. It is non-negotiable.

Payroll

Payroll runs on an automated schedule, so paying the team is never dependent on any one person's bandwidth — it simply runs on time, every time.

Daily Financial Reporting

The team has live visibility into cash and account activity through automated daily reporting — so decisions are made on current numbers, not guesswork.
