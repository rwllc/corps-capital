Financial Execution & Controls

How Money Moves

One person is accountable for getting payments out on time and in the right amount — protected by a clear approval flow and controls.

The Principle

Tom Morgan approves. Jasmine executes. Payments happen on time and in the right amounts — and if an answer isn't known, Jasmine goes to the source (Tino, the custodian, the bank) and resolves it rather than waiting.

The Control Protocol (every wire)

  1. Request logged — who, what, amount, purpose. Documented.
  2. Approval — Tino confirms operational sanity and funds, under controls set by Tom Morgan. For certain transactions, Tom Morgan reserves additional approval by himself. Routed through Jasmine.
  3. Verbal confirmation on a known channel before send.
  4. Execution — Jasmine sends it.
  5. Acknowledgment back to Tom Morgan, and an entry in the daily report.

Payroll

Payroll runs on an automated schedule, so paying the team is never dependent on any one person's bandwidth — it simply runs on time, every time.

Daily Financial Reporting

The team has live visibility into cash and account activity through automated daily reporting — so decisions are made on current numbers, not guesswork.

P&L — Generated, Maintained, Reported

A full profit-and-loss statement is generated, maintained, and reported on a regular cadence — compared against budget and against last year's numbers, so the firm always knows exactly where it stands. Bonnie generates the P&L; Jasmine oversees it.
